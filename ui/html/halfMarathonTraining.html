Half Marathon Training Plan

Week 1-4: Building Base Endurance and Speed

Goal: Establish a solid base of endurance and speed.

Training 1: Long Run

Distance: Start with 8 km and gradually increase to 13 km.

Pace: Comfortable, conversational pace.

Duration: 60-90 minutes.

Tip: Focus on building endurance without worrying about speed.

Training 2: Speed Work / Interval Training

Warm-up: 10-15 minutes of easy running.

Intervals: Start with 4x400m at 5K pace with 2-3 minutes of rest. Increase to 6x800m by the end of week 4.

Cool-down: 10-15 minutes of easy running.

Tip: Focus on maintaining a steady pace for each interval.

Week 5-8: Increasing Mileage and Intensity

Goal: Increase mileage and introduce tempo runs.

Training 1: Long Run

Distance: Increase from 13 km to 16 km.

Pace: Comfortable, conversational pace.

Duration: 90-120 minutes.

Tip: Stay hydrated and fuel appropriately during longer runs.

Training 2: Tempo Run

Warm-up: 10-15 minutes of easy running.

Tempo Segment: Start with 20 minutes at a comfortably hard pace (just below race pace). Gradually increase to 40 minutes.

Cool-down: 10-15 minutes of easy running.

Tip: The tempo pace should feel challenging but sustainable for the duration of the tempo segment.

Week 9-12: Race-Specific Training and Tapering

Goal: Fine-tune race pace and taper for the race.

Training 1: Long Run

Distance: Increase to 19 km by week 10, then taper to 16 km and 13 km in weeks 11 and 12.

Pace: Start incorporating segments at race pace during the long run. For example, run the first half at a comfortable pace and the second half at race pace.

Duration: 90-120 minutes.

Tip: Practice race-day nutrition and hydration strategies.

Training 2: Race Pace Intervals

Warm-up: 10-15 minutes of easy running.

Intervals: Start with 3x1 km at race pace with 3 minutes of rest. Gradually increase to 5x1 km.

Cool-down: 10-15 minutes of easy running.

Tip: Focus on maintaining a consistent pace for each interval, mimicking race conditions.

Week 13: Race Week

Goal: Taper and prepare for race day.

Training 1: Short, Easy Run

Distance: 5-6 km.

Pace: Easy, conversational pace.

Duration: 30-40 minutes.

Tip: Keep the intensity low and focus on staying relaxed.

Training 2: Shakeout Run

Distance: 3-4 km.

Pace: Easy, with a few short race-pace strides.

Duration: 20-30 minutes.

Tip: This run is just to keep your legs fresh.

By following this plan and staying consistent, you should be well-prepared to complete your half marathon in under 2 hours. Good luck with your training!
